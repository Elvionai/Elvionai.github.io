Your characters are the **heart of your story**, the anchors that hold the reader's attention and emotion. A compelling plot is important, but a reader will abandon a book if they don't care about the people experiencing the events. Learning how to build three-dimensional, memorable characters is arguably the most crucial skill for any writer.

Every great character has three driving forces:

  • **Want (External Goal):** What they actively pursue (e.g., getting the treasure, winning the competition).
  • **Need (Internal Goal):** What they truly require for fulfillment (e.g., accepting their vulnerability, forgiving themselves).
  • **The Lie:** The flawed belief or worldview preventing them from achieving their Need (e.g., "I must control everything," "I am unlovable").
The story‚Äôs arc is the journey where the character confronts **The Lie** to fulfill their **Need**. This creates authentic, emotional stakes.

These human inconsistencies make them leap off the page.
