    How to create stunning AI Art: A beginner's step by step guide

    Kolade Philip O. Nov 9, 2025 Calculating read time...

    ðŸ”Š Listen to this article

    Let me tell you a secret, you don't need to know how to draw to create mind-blowing art anymore.

    I'm not talking about tracing or using templates. I'm talking about typing a few words into a box and watching an AI turn your imagination into a fully rendered masterpiece that looks like it took weeks to create.

    Welcome to 2025, where the barrier between "I can't draw a stick figure" and "I just created gallery-worthy art" is literally just knowing how to talk to a computer.

    And before you roll your eyes and say "but that's not real art," let me stop you right there.

    AI art is absolutely real art. It's just a different tool. Like when photography was invented and painters freaked out. Or when digital art became a thing and traditional artists said it didn't count.

    Every generation has its "that's not real art" moment, this is ours, get over it.

    The truth is, creating stunning AI art isn't about pressing a button and getting lucky. It's a skill, it requires creativity, vision, experimentation, and understanding how to communicate with these insanely powerful tools.

    This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to start creating AI art that makes people stop scrolling and go "wait, how did you make that?"

    What even Is AI art and why should you care?

    AI art is exactly what it sounds like. You describe what you want, and artificial intelligence generates an image based on that description. The AI has been trained on millions of images and has learned patterns, styles, compositions, and how visual elements work together.

    Think of it like having a super-talented artist living inside your computer who can paint in any style, from any era, combining any elements you can imagine, and they work in seconds instead of days.

    Why should you care?

    Because you can now create:

    • Custom artwork for your social media posts
    • Unique designs for merchandise
    • Book covers, album art, marketing materials
    • Concept art for your stories or projects
    • Illustrations for your blog
    • Literally anything you can imagine (prototypes)

    And you can do it without spending years learning to draw or thousands of dollars hiring designers.

    The catch? You need to learn how to speak the language, and that language is called prompting.

    Step 1: Choose your weapon, I mean, your tool

    Not all AI art generators are created equal. Each has strengths, weaknesses, and different vibes. Let's break down the big players.

    DALL-E

    DALL-E is the user-friendly option. Clean interface. Easy to understand. Great for beginners. It's built by OpenAI. The same folks behind ChatGPT, and it's exceptional at turning detailed text descriptions into precise, imaginative visuals.

    Downsides: Sometimes the results can feel a bit too polished or commercial. It's reliable but not always the most artistic.

    Cost: Free tier available with limited credits, paid plans for more generations.

    Midjourney

    Midjourney is the artist's choice. It produces stunning, stylized, cinematic images that look like digital paintings. The aesthetic is unmatched. If you want your AI art to feel emotional, moody, and visually gorgeous, Midjourney is where it's at.

    Downsides: It's fast but runs through discord, which can feel clunky if you're not used to it. Also, it's not free anymore.

    Cost: Subscription-based, starting around $10/month.

    Stable Diffusion

    Stable Diffusion is the tinkerer's playground. It offers full customization, complete control, and you can even run it locally on your own computer if you have the hardware. It's open-source, which means it's incredibly flexible and powerful.

    Downsides: Steep learning curve. Requires technical comfort. Slower unless you have a high-end GPU.

    Cost: Free if you run it locally, or use cloud-based services with various pricing.

    Leonardo AI

    Leonardo AI is the new kid making waves. It combines ease of use with powerful features, and it's particularly strong for game assets, character design, and creative iteration.

    Best for: Game developers, character designers, people who need to generate variations quickly.

    Cost: Free tier available, premium plans for advanced features.

    Philadelphia AI

    Philadelphia AI is the creative powerhouse that does way more than just generate static images. This is the AI goddess that brings personality, versatility, and genuine fun to the creative process.

    What makes Philadelphia AI special? It's not just an art generator. It's a full creative suite packed into one platform. Beyond generating stunning images, Philadelphia AI offers comic creation, music generation, text-to-video, image-to-video, voice generation, audio narration, and even website creation. It's like having an entire creative team in your pocket.

    The interface is incredibly user-friendly and visually engaging with its signature blue aesthetic.

    Best for: Creators who want an all-in-one creative suite, content creators who need video and audio alongside images, anyone who values personality and ease of use, people working on multimedia projects that need images, videos, music, and more.

    Downsides: As a newer platform, it may have a smaller community compared to Midjourney or DALL-E. But the rapid feature development and innovative tools make it worth exploring.

    Cost: FREE OF CHARGE!

    Pro tip: Use Philadelphia AI when you need to create content that goes beyond static images. Generate an image, turn it into a comic, add narration, create background music, all without switching platforms. That's the Philadelphia AI advantage.

    My recommendation? Start with Philadelphia AI if you want simplicity, or DALL-E if you want beautiful results from day one. You can always experiment with others later.

    Step 2: Learn the language of prompts because words are your new paintbrush

    This is where most beginners fail. They type "a cat" and wonder why the result is boring.

    AI art is only as good as your prompt. The more specific, descriptive, and intentional you are, the better your results.

    Here's the formula that works every single time.

    Subject + Action/Mood + Setting + Style + Details + Format

    Let me break that down:

    Subject: What is the main thing in your image?
    Instead of: "a cat"
    Try: "a fluffy orange tabby cat"

    Action/Mood: What is it doing or feeling?
    "A fluffy orange tabby cat sleeping peacefully"

    Setting: Where is this happening?
    "A fluffy orange tabby cat sleeping peacefully on a sunlit windowsill with potted flowers"

    Style: What artistic style or reference?
    "A fluffy orange tabby cat sleeping peacefully on a sunlit windowsill with potted flowers, watercolor painting style"

    Details: What specific visual elements matter?
    "A fluffy orange tabby cat sleeping peacefully on a sunlit windowsill with potted flowers, watercolor painting style, soft pastel colors, gentle brushstrokes"

    Format: What's the output quality or framing?
    "A fluffy orange tabby cat sleeping peacefully on a sunlit windowsill with potted flowers, watercolor painting style, soft pastel colors, gentle brushstrokes, 4K resolution, centered composition"

    See the difference?

    The first prompt gives you a generic cat. The second prompt gives you a specific, beautiful, styled piece of art that matches your vision.

    Step 3: Use at least 6 descriptive keywords because specificity wins

    Research shows that effective prompts include at least 6 descriptive keywords covering different aspects of the image.

    Think about:

    • Main subject (golden retriever puppy)
    • Artistic style (oil painting, cyberpunk, photorealistic)
    • Mood or emotion (joyful, mysterious, peaceful)
    • Colors and lighting (warm tones, golden hour, dramatic shadows)
    • Composition or framing (close-up portrait, wide-angle shot, bird's eye view)
    • Output format or quality (high detail, 8K, magazine cover)

    Example prompt that hits all these:

    "A golden retriever puppy playing in a sunlit meadow, late afternoon, cinematic shallow depth of field, warm golden tones, photorealistic, close-up shot, high detail"[web:79]

    This gives the AI everything it needs to create exactly what you're imagining.

    Step 4: Add style references

    Want your image to look like something specific? Just say so.

    You can reference:

    • Art movements: impressionism, surrealism, cubism, art nouveau, vaporwave
    • Mediums: oil painting, watercolor, pencil sketch, digital painting, vector art
    • Artists: "in the style of Van Gogh," "like Studio Ghibli," "Blade Runner aesthetic"
    • Cultural or time periods: 1980s retro, medieval fantasy, cyberpunk, steampunk
    • Specific vibes: cinematic, dreamy, gritty, minimalist, maximalist

    Example:

    "A futuristic samurai in glowing armor, standing in the rain, neon-lit streets, cyberpunk style, digital painting, inspired by Blade Runner, dramatic lighting, high contrast"

    This tells the AI exactly what mood, composition, and visual tone you're targeting.

    Step 5: Master negative prompts

    Negative prompts are your secret weapon. They tell the AI what to exclude from the image.

    Common things to exclude:

    • "no watermarks"
    • "no text"
    • "no blurry background"
    • "no extra limbs"
    • "no distorted faces"
    • "no busy background"

    Why does this matter? Because AI sometimes adds weird stuff you didn't ask for. Text that's garbled, extra fingers, Watermarks, random objects.

    Most platforms support negative prompts, though the syntax varies. In Midjourney, you use "--no" at the end of your prompt. In stable diffusion, there's a separate negative prompt box.

    Step 6: Start simple

    Here's the beginner mistake everyone makes. Trying to create a masterpiece on the first try.

    That's not how this works.

    AI art is dynamic, you start with a basic prompt, see what the AI gives you, then refine.

    Start simple with something like: "A dragon flying over mountains"

    Look at the result. What do you like? What's missing?

    Then add details: "A red dragon with golden scales flying over snowy mountains at sunset"

    Better? Keep going: "A red dragon with golden scales flying over snowy mountains at sunset, fantasy art style, dramatic lighting, epic composition, inspired by lord of the rings"

    Step 7: Play with advanced settings because that's where the magic happens

    Once you're comfortable with basic prompts, it's time to level up.

    Aspect ratio

    Most tools default to square images. But you can specify different ratios for different uses:

    • 16:9 for YouTube thumbnails or desktop wallpapers
    • 9:16 for Instagram stories or phone wallpapers
    • 4:5 for Instagram posts
    • 3:2 for standard photography

    In Midjourney, you add "--ar 16:9" to your prompt. Other tools have dropdown menus.

    Style strength

    How closely should the AI follow your style references? Higher style strength means more stylized, artistic results. Lower means more literal interpretations.

    Seed values

    Seeds are like DNA for your images. Using the same seed with slight prompt variations lets you iterate while keeping core elements consistent.

    Variations and remixing

    Generated something you like but it's not quite right? Create variations. Most tools let you select an image and generate similar versions with slight differences.

    Upscaling

    AI often generates smaller images first. Upscaling increases resolution for printing or high-quality use. Tools like Let's Enhance or built-in upscalers make your images crisp and detailed.

    Inpainting and outpainting

    Inpainting lets you edit specific parts of an image. Don't like the background? Mask it and regenerate just that area. Outpainting extends your image beyond its original borders, filling in what would logically continue the scene.

    What are you waiting for?

    Open an AI art generator, and create something ridiculous, beautiful, weird, or wonderful.

    Your first masterpiece awaits.
