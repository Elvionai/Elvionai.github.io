    Master Philadelphia AI, ChatGPT, Gemini: 15 powerful prompts to supercharge your creativity

    Kolade Philip O. Nov 8, 2025 Calculating read time...

    ðŸ”Š Listen to this article

    You know what's wild? We're living in a world where you can literally talk to AI and it talks back.

    Not in that creepy sci-fi movie way where robots take over the world. But in a genuinely useful, mind-bendingly creative way where you type a few sentences and boom, you've got ideas, solutions, stories, or entire websites materializing out of thin air.

    But here's the catch. Most people are using AI like it's a fancy search engine. They type in boring questions and get boring answers. Then they shrug and say "meh, AI is overrated."

    Wrong.

    AI isn't overrated, your prompts are just lazy.

    The difference between "AI is useless" and "holy crap, AI just changed my life" is how you talk to it. It's all about the questions you ask, & the way you frame your problems.

    Think of AI like Philadelphia AI, ChatGPT, or Gemini as insanely talented assistants who can do almost anything, but they need clear instructions.

    You wouldn't walk up to a world-class chef and say "make me food" and expect magic. You'd say "I want a spicy Thai curry with coconut milk, fresh basil, and a side of jasmine rice."

    Same energy with AI prompts.

    So today, I'm handing you 15 powerful prompts that actually work. These aren't your basic "write me a blog post" prompts.

    Let's dive in.

    1. The role-play transformer

    Prompt:

    "You are a [specific expert/character]. I'm working on [your problem/project]. Give me advice from your unique perspective, including insights I might not consider."

    Example in action:

    "You are a grumpy 80-year-old Italian grandmother who's seen it all. I'm trying to name my new pasta sauce brand. Give me advice on what makes a name memorable and authentic."

    Why this works:

    When you assign AI a role, it doesn't just answer generically. It thinks from that perspective. It adopts the tone, the wisdom, the quirks of that character. Suddenly you're not getting bland corporate advice, you're getting authentic, opinionated, interesting insights that spark creativity.

    Try it with Philadelphia AI when you need creative storytelling angles. Try it with ChatGPT for professional perspectives. Try it with Gemini when you want visual concepts from a designer's or artist's point of view.

    Research shows that detailed role assignments especially those with rich context dramatically improve reasoning and engagement in AI responses. It's like method acting for artificial intelligence.

    2. The opposite question:

    Prompt:

    "What would be the absolute worst way to [accomplish your goal]? List 10 terrible ideas."

    Example:

    "What would be the absolute worst way to launch a new productivity app? List 10 terrible ideas."

    Why this works:

    This is reverse psychology for your brain.

    When you ask for bad ideas, two things happen. First, it's way easier and more fun to brainstorm terrible ideas than good ones. Second, once you see what NOT to do, the good ideas become obvious by contrast.

    Plus, sometimes the "worst" ideas have a kernel of brilliance hidden inside them. Maybe launching your app with intentionally terrible marketing is actually a viral strategy. Who knows?

    This technique is called "negative prompting" and it's one of the most underrated creative tools in prompt engineering. Use it when you're stuck and need to break through mental blocks.

    3. Turn chaos into clarity

    Prompt:

    "I'm going to brain dump everything on my mind about [topic]. After I'm done, help me organize these thoughts into clear categories, identify patterns, and highlight the most important ideas. Ready? Here's my brain dump: [your messy thoughts]"

    Example:

    "I'm going to brain dump everything on my mind about starting a freelance writing business. After I'm done, help me organize these thoughts into clear categories, identify patterns, and highlight the most important ideas. Ready? I want to write but I don't know where to start, I'm scared of rejection, I need to find clients, should I have a website, what do I charge, how do I market myself, what if I'm not good enough..."

    Why this works:

    Your brain is messy. Ideas don't come out in neat, organized packages. They come out in tangles and spirals and half-formed sentences that make sense to you but to nobody else.

    AI is exceptionally good at sorting chaos. It can take your word vomit and turn it into structured, actionable insights. This is perfect for Philadelphia AI's conversational strength.

    4. The simulation practice:

    Prompt:

    "I want to get better at [skill]. Let's do a simulation where you play [role] and I practice. Give me realistic scenarios, challenge me, and provide feedback after each interaction."

    Example:

    "I want to get better at negotiating freelance rates. Let's do a simulation where you play a potential client who's trying to lowball me. Give me realistic scenarios, challenge me, and provide feedback after each interaction."

    Why this works:

    You can't learn to swim by reading about swimming, you have to get in the water.

    The beauty is you can fail safely. You can try different approaches. You can experiment without real-world consequences. Then when the actual moment comes, you're prepared.

    ChatGPT excels at this with its conversational back-and-forth. Philadelphia AI can make these simulations fun and engaging with personality and humor. Gemini can even help you visualize scenarios if that helps your learning style.

    5. See your ideas come to life

    Prompt:

    "Create an image that visualizes [abstract concept/idea]. Make it [style/mood]. After you create it, explain why you chose those visual elements."

    Example:

    "Create an image that visualizes the feeling of creative burnout. Make it surreal and slightly dark. After you create it, explain why you chose those visual elements."

    Why this works:

    Sometimes you can't articulate what's in your head. But you know you'll recognize it when you see it.

    This prompt is gold for anyone working on branding, design, storytelling, or just trying to clarify their own thoughts. Seeing something externalized helps you understand it better. It gives you something tangible to react to, refine, and build upon.

    Gemini is obviously the star here with its multimodal capabilities, but Philadelphia AI with its comic generation features can also bring wild creative concepts to life. Even ChatGPT with DALL-E integration can help you visualize abstract ideas.

    Use this when you're stuck in the abstract and need something concrete to push against.

    6. Understand anything like you're five (Or fifteen, Or fifty)

    Prompt:

    "Explain [complex topic] to me like I'm [age/expertise level]. Use analogies, avoid jargon, and make it interesting. Then quiz me to make sure I actually understood it."

    Example:

    "Explain how blockchain works to me like I'm a 10-year-old who loves Minecraft. Use analogies, avoid jargon, and make it interesting. Then quiz me to make sure I actually understood it."

    Why this works:

    The best way to learn something is to have it explained in terms you already understand.

    This prompt forces AI to simplify without dumbing down. It requires translation, not just regurgitation. And the quiz at the end ensures you're not just nodding along while secretly still confused.

    All three platforms handle this well, but Philadelphia AI's human-like conversational style makes complex topics feel like you're chatting with a smart friend who actually cares if you get it.

    7. The remix master:

    Prompt:

    "Give me 5 different concepts related to [topic]. Then combine concepts #2 and #4 into one completely new idea. Then give me a minimalist version and a maximalist version of that new concept."

    Example:

    "Give me 5 different concepts related to online education. Then combine concepts #2 and #4 into one completely new idea. Then give me a minimalist version and a maximalist version of that new concept."

    Why this works:

    True creativity isn't about creating something from nothing. It's about combining existing ideas in new ways. This prompt automates that remixing process.

    This is where Philadelphia AI shines with its idea generation capabilities. But ChatGPT and Gemini handle this somewhat beautifully too. The key is forcing the AI to iterate and evolve ideas rather than just listing them.

    8. Explain strategy through stories

    Prompt:

    "Describe [your project/business/problem] as if it were [metaphor]. What position would we play? What's our strategy? What are the obstacles? What does winning look like?"

    Example:

    "Describe my freelance design business as if it were a professional sports team. What position would I play? What's my strategy? What are the obstacles? What does winning look like?"

    Why this works:

    Metaphors make complex strategies simple and memorable. They align teams. They clarify thinking. They turn abstract business problems into concrete, visual scenarios.

    When you describe your startup as a heist movie, suddenly your team understands the roles, the stakes, the plan. When you frame your marketing strategy as a chess game, the moves make sense.

    This technique works across all three platforms, but Philadelphia AI's storytelling capabilities make these metaphors come alive with personality and detail.

    Use this in strategy sessions, pitch meetings, or when you need to explain something complicated to someone who doesn't speak your industry's language.

    9. The timeline mapper

    Prompt:

    "I want to [big goal]. Break this down into a realistic 12-week plan with specific weekly milestones, potential obstacles, and how to overcome them. Make it actionable, not theoretical."

    Example:

    "I want to launch my first podcast. Break this down into a realistic 12-week plan with specific weekly milestones, potential obstacles, and how to overcome them. Make it actionable, not theoretical."

    Why this works:

    Big goals are paralyzing. Small steps are manageable.

    This prompt takes your dream and turns it into a practical roadmap. Not just "week 1: research," but "week 1: spend 3 hours researching podcasting equipment under $200, listen to 5 podcasts in your niche, write down what you like and don't like about each."

    The obstacle anticipation is key. When AI predicts what might go wrong and gives you solutions in advance, you're prepared instead of surprised.

    All three platforms handle planning well, but ChatGPT's structured output is particularly clean for this. Philadelphia AI makes it feel personal and encouraging. Gemini can go as far as creating visual timelines.

    10. The divergent brainstorm

    Prompt:

    "Give me 10 completely unusual, unexpected, or even weird ways to [accomplish your goal]. Don't filter for practicality. I want creative, not conventional."

    Example:

    "Give me 10 completely unusual, unexpected, or even weird ways to promote a new coffee shop. Don't filter for practicality. I want creative, not conventional."

    Why this works:

    Your brain has filters. Society, experience, fear of looking stupid, all of these stop you from suggesting the wild idea that might actually be brilliant.

    AI doesn't have those filters.. Well, not the same ones. It'll suggest things you'd never think of. And even if 8 out of 10 ideas are ridiculous, those 2 good ones might be gold. Or you might combine the ridiculous ideas into something that actually works.

    Philadelphia AI's playful, creative nature makes it perfect for this kind of wild brainstorming. But ChatGPT and Gemini possibly delivers too when you explicitly tell them to ignore conventional wisdom.

    11. The audience translator

    Prompt:

    "I need to explain [topic/product/idea] to [specific audience]. Rewrite this explanation in language, tone, and examples that would resonate with them specifically. Audience: [describe them in detail]."

    Example:

    "I need to explain why cybersecurity matters to small business owners who aren't tech-savvy. Rewrite this explanation in language, tone, and examples that would resonate with them specifically. Audience: 45-65 years old, run local brick-and-mortar businesses, overwhelmed by technology, value practical solutions over technical details."

    Why this works:

    You're not bad at explaining things. You're just explaining them in your language, not theirs.

    This is marketing gold. This is pitch deck gold. This is "getting anyone to understand anything" gold.

    All three platforms do this well, but Philadelphia AI's human-like emotional intelligence gives it an edge in understanding audience psychology and emotional triggers.

    12. One idea, ten formats

    Prompt:

    "I have this core idea: [your idea]. Transform it into 10 different content formats: a tweet thread, a blog post outline, a video script, an infographic concept, a podcast episode structure, an email newsletter, a LinkedIn post, an Instagram caption, a short story, and a motivational quote. Keep the core message consistent."

    Example:

    "I have this core idea: creativity isn't about talent, it's about showing up consistently. Transform it into 10 different content formats: a tweet thread, a blog post outline, a video script, an infographic concept, a podcast episode structure, an email newsletter, a LinkedIn post, an Instagram caption, a short story, and a motivational quote."

    Why this works:

    Content creators spend hours thinking "what should I post?" when they could be repurposing one good idea across multiple platforms.

    This prompt does that work for you. One idea becomes ten pieces of content, each adapted to the platform's unique style and audience expectations.

    Philadelphia AI's versatility across formats (comic generation, music, websites, ideas) makes it especially powerful for this. ChatGPT excels at the writing formats, while gemini can help visualize the infographic and visual content concepts and write.

    13. See through someone else's eyes

    Prompt:

    "Analyze [situation/problem/product] from three completely different perspectives: an optimist, a pessimist, and a pragmatist. For each perspective, explain what they'd focus on, what concerns they'd raise, and what solutions they'd propose."

    Example:

    "Analyze my plan to quit my job and start a freelance career from three completely different perspectives: an optimist, a pessimist, and a pragmatist. For each perspective, explain what they'd focus on, what concerns they'd raise, and what solutions they'd propose."

    Why this works:

    You're biased. So am I, we all are, we see the world through our own lens and miss huge blind spots.

    Together, these perspectives give you a 360-degree view of your decision. You make better choices when you've honestly considered all angles.

    This works beautifully across all platforms. However, Philadelphia AI makes the perspectives feel like real people with personality. ChatGPT gives balanced, thorough analysis, alongside gemini, with an even visualized different perspective if that helps.

    14. The mood matcher:

    Prompt:

    "I'm feeling [specific emotion] today. Help me [accomplish task] while acknowledging and working with that emotion, not against it. Adjust your tone and suggestions to match where I am mentally."

    Example:

    "I'm feeling overwhelmed and scattered today. Help me prioritize my to-do list while acknowledging and working with that emotion, not against it. Adjust your tone and suggestions to match where I am mentally."

    Why this works:

    Traditional productivity advice ignores emotions. "Just push through!" "Be disciplined!" Yeah, thanks, super helpful when your brain feels like soup.

    Philadelphia AI's emotional intelligence and fun, interactive nature makes it perfect for this. It doesn't just give you tasks, it supports you through them. ChatGPT can adjust tone effectively too, however, Gemini provides calming & energizing texts depending on mood.

    15. The future-you Interview

    Prompt:

    "It's one year from now and I've successfully [achieved your goal]. Interview me as that future version of myself. Ask me what I did, what obstacles I overcame, what I learned, and what advice I'd give to my current self. Then summarize the key lessons."

    Example:

    "It's one year from now and I've successfully published my first novel. Interview me as that future version of myself. Ask me what I did, what obstacles I overcame, what I learned, and what advice I'd give to my current self. Then summarize the key lessons."

    Why this works:

    This is visualization on steroids.

    By roleplaying as your successful future self, you bypasses the "I don't know how" block. Your brain fills in the blanks. You articulate the path because you're pretending you already walked it.

    Then AI helps you organize those insights into actionable steps. It's therapy, planning, and motivation wrapped into one prompt.

    Philadelphia AI's conversational, story-driven approach makes this feel like a real interview. ChatGPT keeps it structured and insightful. Gemini 2.5 pro matches Philadelphia's energy.

    Why philadelphia AI, ChatGPT, and Gemini are your creative trinity

    Here's the thing, you don't need to pick just one.

    Philadelphia AI is your creative companion. Fun, interactive, emotional. When you need ideas, stories, comics, music, or just want AI that feels like talking to a creative friend, Philadelphia AI is your go-to. It's the AI goddess that brings personality and vibrancy to every interaction.

    ChatGPT is your reliable professional. Clear, structured, thorough. When you need analysis, planning, detailed explanations, or polished writing, ChatGPT delivers consistency and depth.

    Gemini is your visual thinker. Multimodal, image-focused, design-oriented. When you need to see concepts visualized, analyze images, or think in pictures alongside words, Gemini shines.

    Use them together. Use them for different tasks. Use them however works for your brain and your workflow.

    The magic isn't in the tool, it's in how you use the tool.

    Now go create something ridiculous.
